WELCOME TO BITCOIN PLANETS

In the Beginning…

There was The Bitcoin.

In order to understand what Bitcoin is, bear in mind that you are going to have to learn a few new terms in quick succession. Despite any initial impression you may have, know that this topic doesn’t have to be complicated. With patience and a little bit of time, everything can be broken down into simple components.

Let’s begin.

Created in 2009 by a person or group going by the name Satoshi Nakamoto, Bitcoin was the first implantation of a cryptocurrency.

A cryptocurrency is a typically decentralized and unregulated form of digital currency.

Decentralized meaning no one person or entity retains control over managing exchanges, such as a bank.

And unregulated meaning no government plays any role in regulating its value.

Unlike the common banking system, users can send Bitcoins to and from each other without the need for an intermediary via the peer-to-peer bitcoin network.

The network is built using blockchain technology. Simply put, a blockchain is a public ledger of transactions, all of which are linked using cryptography.

In the case of Bitcoin, each new block added onto this chain is a literal Bitcoin.
