Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a digital form of currency. Unlike regular money, it is not typically issued by a central authority (like the Federal Reserve for the U.S. dollar). This is all made possible by a technology known as the BlockChain.

Alt coins-

The rise of Bitcoin inspired the creation of many other cryptocurrencies, known as altcoins. Currently there are over 9000 altcoins, accounting for 40% of the total cryptocurrency market in March 2021. Altcoins typically share many characteristics with Bitcoin, but can be different in certain respects.

Wallets-

A bitcoin wallet is a software program that functions as a digital wallet. Payments can be made and received via the wallet. It holds a user's private keys, which correspond to bitcoins on the blockchain. The wallet itself has its own private key, which functions as an address to receive payments.

Hard Wallets-

A hard wallet is a designated piece of hardware for securely storing a user’s private keys. It’s primary advantages over a regular (digital) wallet are: the lack of exposure the private keys face to malicious hackers, pin encryption, and immunity to computer viruses.

Exchanges - buying selling

Cryptocurrencies can be bought and sold on crypto exchanges, which are digital marketplaces where traders can buy and sell cryptocurrencies using fiat currency or altcoins.

Futures-

Similar to how one can invest in stocks using a futures contract, one may do the same with crypto. This allows for investors to speculate on the price of a cryptocurrency and eliminates the need of having to set up a wallet, which can act for some as a barrier to entry.

Stablecoins-

Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies that attempt to match their price to a fiat currency.

Investopedia

Crypto Lending & Borrowing-

Crypto lending is an alternative investment strategy, where lenders can receive interest on money lent (fiat or crypto) by utilizing smart contracts. A borrower leaves a collateral (typically a cryptocurrency) and may then borrow up to the value of the collateral. The borrowed money is usually in the form of stable coins. Once the borrower pays back the loan (along with the agreed upon interest rate), their collateral is unfrozen. In this way, any change of value of the cryptocurrency collateral is a stake that the borrower retains (for better and worse).
